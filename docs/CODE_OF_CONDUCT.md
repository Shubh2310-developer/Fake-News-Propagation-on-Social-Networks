# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Fake News Game Theory research community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on advancing scientific research and combating misinformation through rigorous academic inquiry.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

###  Encouraged Behaviors

- **Respectful Communication**: Using welcoming and inclusive language in all interactions
- **Constructive Feedback**: Providing thoughtful, actionable feedback on research and code
- **Academic Integrity**: Maintaining high standards of scientific rigor and ethical research practices
- **Collaborative Spirit**: Working together to advance our understanding of fake news propagation
- **Empathy and Understanding**: Showing consideration for different perspectives and experiences
- **Recognition of Contributions**: Acknowledging the work and ideas of all contributors appropriately
- **Professional Conduct**: Maintaining professionalism in all research and development activities
- **Open-minded Learning**: Being receptive to new ideas, methodologies, and research approaches
- **Responsible Disclosure**: Following proper channels for reporting bugs, vulnerabilities, or concerns

### Research-Specific Standards

- **Reproducibility**: Ensuring research results can be replicated by others
- **Data Ethics**: Respecting privacy and ethical considerations in data usage
- **Methodological Transparency**: Clearly documenting research methods and assumptions
- **Peer Review**: Engaging constructively in the peer review process
- **Citation Practices**: Properly attributing sources, datasets, and prior work

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

### L Prohibited Behaviors

- **Harassment and Discrimination**: Any form of harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary behavior
- **Personal Attacks**: Insulting, derogatory comments, or personal attacks against individuals
- **Trolling and Disruption**: Deliberately provocative, off-topic, or disruptive behavior
- **Inappropriate Content**: Sharing sexually explicit, violent, or otherwise inappropriate material
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Professional Misconduct**: Misrepresenting qualifications, plagiarizing, or falsifying results
- **Spam and Self-promotion**: Excessive self-promotion or spam unrelated to the project
- **Intimidation**: Threatening, stalking, or intimidating behavior toward any community member
- **Unwelcome Advances**: Unwelcome sexual attention or romantic advances

### Research Misconduct

- **Data Manipulation**: Falsifying, fabricating, or misrepresenting research data
- **Plagiarism**: Using others' work, ideas, or code without proper attribution
- **Conflicts of Interest**: Failing to disclose relevant conflicts of interest
- **Ethical Violations**: Conducting research that violates ethical guidelines or human subjects protections
- **Academic Dishonesty**: Any form of cheating, fraud, or deception in academic work

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Reporting Process

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, documentation, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- Communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, both online and offline, including:

- **GitHub repositories** and related discussions
- **Academic conferences** and presentations featuring this work
- **Research collaborations** and partnerships
- **Social media** when representing the project
- **Email communications** related to the project
- **Virtual and in-person meetings** or events

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

=ç **conduct@fake-news-game-theory.org**

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

1. **Description of the incident** with specific details
2. **Date and time** of occurrence
3. **Location/platform** where it occurred
4. **Individuals involved** (without compromising privacy)
5. **Any supporting evidence** (screenshots, links, etc.)
6. **Impact on you or others**
7. **Desired resolution** if applicable

### Response Process

All complaints will be:

- **Reviewed promptly** within 48-72 hours
- **Investigated fairly** and thoroughly
- **Handled confidentially** with discretion
- **Resolved appropriately** based on severity and circumstances

The reporting person will be notified of the outcome and any actions taken.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Research Ethics Addendum

### Academic Integrity

As a research-focused project, we hold ourselves to additional standards:

- **Honest Reporting**: All research findings must be reported honestly and accurately
- **Proper Attribution**: All sources, datasets, and collaborators must be properly credited
- **Conflict Disclosure**: Any potential conflicts of interest must be transparently disclosed
- **Data Responsibility**: Researchers must handle data ethically and in compliance with relevant regulations
- **Collaborative Respect**: Research collaborations must be conducted with mutual respect and clear agreements

### Human Subjects and Privacy

- **Privacy Protection**: Personal data must be handled with utmost care and respect
- **Informed Consent**: Any research involving human subjects must obtain proper consent
- **Data Minimization**: Collect and use only the minimum data necessary for research purposes
- **Secure Storage**: Implement appropriate security measures for sensitive data

## Appeals Process

If you disagree with an enforcement decision:

1. **Submit a written appeal** to conduct@fake-news-game-theory.org within 14 days
2. **Include detailed reasoning** for why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. **Provide any additional evidence** that supports your position
4. **Appeals will be reviewed** by a different set of community leaders
5. **Final decisions** will be communicated within 30 days
